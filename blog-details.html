Award for Excellence in Innovation and Technology Driven Enterprise

  • Jun 27, 2022
  • 12 Comments

Phoenix Renewables was given an award for excellence in innovation and technology driven enterprise at the 5th MSME awards which took place at the Presidential Villa, Abuja. The award was received by the MD of Phoenix Renewables Mustapha Abubakar Gajibo alongside the CEO Sadiq Abubakar Isah and other members of staff.

In his acceptance speech the MD thanked the MSME clinic for finding Phoenix Renewables worthy of such an award and assured it will only push to even put in more effort until Nigeria is saturated with electric cars. The Future is indeed GREEN.

The Phoenix Renewables team led by the MD. Mustapha Abubakar Gajibo officially presented the pioneer set of Electric Buses produced by the company based in Borno State to the Executive Governor.

The Phoenix Renewables team led by the MD. Mustapha Abubakar Gajibo officially presented the pioneer set of electric Buses produced by the company based in Borno state to the executive Governor of Borno state Prof. Babagana Umara Zulum at the Government House Maiduguri.

While showcasing the Bus, Mustapha told the governor the importance of the Electric Buses to the climate and it been a substitute for mechanical vehicles. The bus has the capacity to travel 100 to 150 kilometres before requiring recharge as they are 100% electric. With this it will go a long way to cushion the effects of fuel subsidy removal by next year, the buses will be used for intra state transport with the aim of revolutionising the transport system in Maiduguri Metropolis and the country at large.

The Governor who was visibly excited and overwhelmed by such novel innovation within his state promised to do everything within his power to make sure the project works and promised that the state government will heavily invest in it. He retreirated that that the electric buses are fully inline with his administration green policy. He assured that he will also make sure that buses are hundred percent produced in the state.

The Honorable Commissioner Ministry of transportation Borno state Dr. Abubakar Tijjani visits the Phoenix renewables workshop to see how the electric cars are being manufactured.

  • Visitation

The Honorable Commissioner Ministry of transportation Borno state Dr. Abubakar Tijjani visits the Phoenix renewables workshop to see how the electric cars are being manufactured. The Phoenix Renewables team led by the CEO Eng. Sadik Abubakar and the MD Mustapha Abubakar Gajibo received him and went round to brief on how the processes are being done.

The MD explained the whole operations of the company about how the buses are transformed from normal conventional motors to fully electrical motors, also explained how the mechanical Engines are transformed into Hybrid gas generators in order not to waste the parts. The MD further explain to the commissioner that with this innovation the transportation system in Borno state will rise to world-class modern means of transportation. The MD also used this opportunity to showcase the Bus that the company is creating from scratch level.

He also explains that apart from the conversion of the Buses, charging stations will be built across the Maiduguri metropolis which will be able to sustain the activities of Buses and will also create job opportunities to individuals across the state. In his part, the Commissioner assured that his ministry will try to reach out not only to the Borno State Government but also to the federal level to meet up the demand.

Phoenix Renewables limited presented the 7-seater electric Bus designed and transformed from fossil fuel to 100% electric by the company in Maiduguri to the National Automotive Design and Development (NADDC) at its corporate headquarters in Abuja.

  • Sept 3rd, 2022
  • Presentation
  • 83 Comments

Phoenix Renewables limited presented the 7-seater electric Bus designed and transformed from fossil fuel to 100% electric by the company in Maiduguri to the National Automotive Design and Development (NADDC) at its corporate headquarters in Abuja. The Director-General NADDC Jelani Aliyu applauded the innovators for adding more value to the local automobile industry in the country. The DG added that this is a significant development having young talents understanding challenges and being aware of where the world is heading especially now that the world is talking about Renewable Energy.

The CEO Phoenix Renewables, Sadiq Abubakar and the MD Mustapha Abubakar Gajibo solicited the assistance of the NADDC and support to enable the company to establish a Mega factory that will enable the company to produce more than 4,000 electric vehicles per annum. The CEO also explained that Phoenix Renewables is trying to make the product of electric vehicles accessible to civil servants without having to pay 100% cost of the vehicle before owning one and also the Massive investment opportunity that the mass transit bus presents for corporate organizations and individuals to enable them make money out of the electric city transit over time.

Furthermore, the technical team of the NADDC and Phoenix Renewables will set up critical structures for a further development to ensure that the electric vehicles becomes the new normal in Nigeria. The NADDC assured of collaboration with Phoenix Renewables to explore the development of this innovation through building infrastructures and development of charging stations, research and development department, and incentive policy development.
